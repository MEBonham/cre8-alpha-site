Glossary

A

Adventure
A significant "chapter" of the game's story.
Adventurer
A person who makes a career out of going on dangerous adventures. The typical life occupation of PCs.
Adventuring Party
A term for the PCs as a collective, especially in campaign settings in which they are recognized by other characters as "adventurers."

B

Backstory
Stuff that happened before the game's story starts. This can especially refer to the personal history of a PC.

C

Campaign
A complete "story" of an RPG is referred to as a campaign. Campaigns therefore vary widely in scope. A campaign may take a few hours to play, or it may last for months or even years.
Campaign Setting
The world (or universe, or even "multiverse") in which a campaign takes place. In CRE8, it is a fantasy world, although in other RPGs, it could be a science fiction universe, a realistic setting, or something more unique. More than one campaign can take place in the same setting, even among the same players; in fact, this is often true of the most successful settings.

Game System
The set of rules that governs an RPG. Typically, the same ruleset is used for an entire campaign.

Half

L

Limited Skills
When a special ability (usually for monsters) "limits" one or more Skills, it means that the number of ranks taken in those skills cannot exceed a character's Heroic Bonus.

M

N

NdS
NPC
Standing for "non-player character," this is a short descriptor for any character who is not a PC, i.e. any character under the GM's control.

O

P

Party
See Adventuring Party
PC
Playgroup
All of the RPG's participants, including the GM (who is not included in "the players").

Q

R

Rule Zero
A quick term for the principle that the GM can override any of the game's normal rules in the interest of making the story better. This may work in the PCs' favor, or it may work against them; it's all up to the GM. GMs, please use this authority responsibly; it is intended to make people have more fun, not as a power trip. Also, in many playgroups, it goes over better if you openly acknowledge that your are invoking "Rule Zero," rather than pretending that you are following the game's normal rules.

S

Session
One continuous "block" of playing an RPG is called a session. A session typically lasts for several hours.
